Signal Reference · GEX Engine v6
Every row in *_gex_history.csv is a 60-second snapshot of the live options chain. Each signal below is a strike price — not a value — indicating where a specific Greek extreme, flow event, or structural level occurred at that moment in time. The chart plots these strikes as scatter points on the price axis so you can see how dealer positioning and order flow evolve through the session.
GEX — Gamma Exposure (Open Interest)
MAXGEX
Dominant Positive GEX Strike
What it is: The strike with the largest positive GEX value across the whole chain — the peak of dealer long-gamma positioning.

Interpretation: Dealers are long gamma here. Above this strike they sell into rallies; below they buy dips. Acts as a magnetic ceiling — price tends to get pinned or repelled. Strong positive GEX environments produce mean-reverting, low-vol tape.
GEX = (Gamma_Call × OI_Call − Gamma_Put × OI_Put) × 100 × Spot² × 0.01
MAXGEX = strike where GEX is maximum if |max| ≥ |min|, else = 0
MINGEX
Dominant Negative GEX Strike
What it is: The strike with the most negative GEX — where puts overwhelm calls in dealer books.

Interpretation: Dealers are short gamma here. They must sell into drops and buy into rips — amplifying moves. Negative GEX environments are volatile: gaps, fast trends, spikes. MINGEX acts as a gravitational accelerant when breached.
MINGEX = strike where GEX is minimum if |min| > |max|, else = 0
Only one of MAXGEX / MINGEX is non-zero per cycle — whichever dominates.
MAXVEX
Dominant Positive VEX Strike
What it is: Same formula as GEX but using intraday volume instead of open interest. The strike where call-side gamma-weighted volume dominates today's flow.

Interpretation: Reveals today's dealer hedging pressure, not the static OI book. MAXVEX is more reactive than MAXGEX — it moves with real-time flow. A MAXVEX close to spot with high volume signals strong call pinning or a local ceiling being actively traded.
VEX = (Gamma_Call × Vol_Call − Gamma_Put × Vol_Put) × 100 × Spot² × 0.01
MINVEX
Dominant Negative VEX Strike
What it is: Strike where put-side gamma-weighted volume dominates. The intraday equivalent of MINGEX.

Interpretation: Where today's put flow is concentrating. If MINVEX is near spot and moving lower during the session, it suggests active put accumulation — dealers are getting shorter gamma in real time and may accelerate a breakdown.
DEX — Delta Exposure
MAXDEX
Maximum Delta Exposure Strike
What it is: The strike with the highest total delta exposure from open interest — where dealers carry the most directional hedge.

Interpretation: High delta concentration means dealers must hold large stock/futures positions here. MAXDEX marks a structural level where the hedging flow is heaviest — often coincides with major resistance or a liquidity magnet.
DEX = (Delta_Call × OI_Call + Delta_Put × OI_Put) × 100
Puts have negative delta, so net DEX blends both sides.
MINDEX
Minimum Delta Exposure Strike
What it is: The strike where total delta exposure is most negative — where put delta dominates and dealers are net short delta.

Interpretation: A strong MINDEX well below spot suggests a heavy put wall. Dealers are long puts here and short stock as a hedge. If price drops toward MINDEX, their buying to cover creates a floor; if it breaks through, the cascade can be violent.
ZERO — Gamma Flip Level
ZERO
VEX Zero-Cross (Volume-Based Flip)
What it is: The interpolated strike price where the VEX curve crosses zero — the point where call gamma-weighted volume and put gamma-weighted volume are balanced. This is the intraday gamma flip, computed from today's volume flow.

Interpretation: The most important structural level in the chart.
  • Price above ZERO → positive gamma regime. Dealers stabilize the market. Expect mean reversion, compressed ranges, fades work better.
  • Price below ZERO → negative gamma regime. Dealers amplify moves. Trends and momentum work better; volatility expands.
  • ZERO moving toward spot intraday → the regime is shifting. Watch for a volatility regime change on a confirmed cross.
Algorithm: finds the first sign-change in individual VEX values (ascending strikes)
starting at ATM — interpolates exact cross between the two bracketing strikes.
Note: uses per-strike VEX values, not cumulative sum.
FLOW — Real-Time Order Classification (ATM)
Tick Classification Logic: Each 60s, the engine compares the last traded price of an option against its bid/ask spread to determine whether the trade was buyer- or seller-initiated. Only ATM strikes (within the configured window) crossing the volume threshold are reported.
CALL BUYlast ≥ bid + spread × 0.6— buyer paid near the ask
CALL SELLlast ≤ bid + spread × 0.4— seller hit the bid
PUT BUYlast ≤ bid + spread × 0.4— inverse rule: put prices rise when bought
PUT SELLlast ≥ bid + spread × 0.6— put seller abandons cheaply
NEUTRAL zone (between thresholds) → volume split 50/50 between buy and sell.
SIGNAL SYMBOL MEANING & MARKET INTERPRETATION
BAC ▲ Cyan triangle-up Buy Aggressor — Calls. An ATM call was bought at or near the ask this cycle. Bullish directional intent. The strike printed is where the aggressive buyer acted. BULLISH
SAC ▲ Purple triangle-up Sell Aggressor — Calls. An ATM call was sold at or near the bid. Could be a covered call, profit-take on a long call, or a short call initiation. Context matters. BEARISH or NEUTRAL
BAP ▼ Cyan triangle-down Buy Aggressor — Puts. An ATM put was bought aggressively. Protective hedge or outright bearish speculation. Strong signal when volume exceeds threshold near a key level. BEARISH
SAP ▼ Purple triangle-down Sell Aggressor — Puts. An ATM put was sold at or near the ask (put seller = bullish). Common during systematic put-selling or hedging unwinds. BULLISH or NEUTRAL
Reading BAC/SAC/BAP/SAP together: Look for confluence. A BAC signal followed by another BAC at the same strike, with no offsetting SAC, shows sustained aggressive call buying — a strong bullish tell. Alternating BAC/SAC at the same strike suggests a two-sided market and less directional conviction. BAP and SAP together near a support level can signal a put spread being legged.
CP — Net Volume Imbalance
MAXCP
Maximum Call/Put Imbalance (Bullish)
What it is: The strike with the highest raw net volume today: Vol_Call − Vol_Put. Where calls are most dominant relative to puts in raw volume terms.

Interpretation: A call-heavy strike. Market participants are placing disproportionate call volume here. Can act as a magnet (gamma pull) or a ceiling (dealer short call hedging).
MAXCP = argmax( Vol_Call[K] − Vol_Put[K] ) across all strikes K
MINCP
Maximum Call/Put Imbalance (Bearish)
What it is: The strike with the most negative net volume: Vol_Call − Vol_Put is most negative here — puts dominate.

Interpretation: A put-heavy strike. Large put volume relative to calls — could signal hedging demand, speculative bearish bets, or a key support level where participants are buying protection.
INVENTORY — Accumulated Intraday Positioning
How inventory works: The engine tracks every delta-volume increment since open. Each tick is classified (buy/sell) and accumulated per strike. These signals are cumulative — they reflect the net flow since the session started, not just the last 60s. The strikes reported are where gamma-weighted inventory is most concentrated.
SIGNAL MEANING & INTERPRETATION
LONGGAMMA Accumulated Long Gamma — Calls + Puts. Strike with the highest combined gamma × bought-volume across both calls and puts since open. Where the market has accumulated the most long-gamma exposure today. A rising LONGGAMMA near spot signals participants are positioned for a squeeze.
SHORTGAMMA Accumulated Short Gamma — Calls + Puts. Strike with the highest gamma × sold-volume. Where sellers have concentrated the most short-gamma exposure. If SHORTGAMMA is near spot and growing, a large breakout could force painful covering.
LONGCALLS Accumulated Long Calls (gamma-weighted). Strike where call buying has been heaviest on a gamma-weighted basis all session. Bullish accumulation zone. Dealers are short these calls and long delta as a hedge — their buying supports price above this level.
SHORTCALLS Accumulated Short Calls (gamma-weighted). Strike where the most calls have been sold. Overhead resistance — dealers are long the sold calls and short delta as a hedge, creating selling pressure on rallies into this level.
LONGPUTS Accumulated Long Puts (gamma-weighted). Strike where put buying is most concentrated all session. Protective demand floor. A rising LONGPUTS signal below spot suggests growing hedge demand — support level if buying is systematic.
SHORTPUTS Accumulated Short Puts (gamma-weighted). Strike where put selling has concentrated. Dealers are long these puts and short delta — they buy on weakness near this level. Acts as a cushion for sharp sell-offs.
SPOT — Price Reference
SPOT
Underlying Price (continuous line)
The underlying index/ETF price, plotted as a continuous white line. This is the anchor reference — all other signals are strikes on the same Y-axis, so you can directly read how far any Greek level is from the current price and watch them converge or diverge in real time.
Reading The Chart — Quick Reference
REGIME DETECTION
SPOT above ZERO → positive gamma regime. Fade extremes, sell vol.

SPOT below ZERO → negative gamma regime. Follow momentum, buy vol.

ZERO crossing SPOT → regime flip. High-probability volatility expansion event.
STRUCTURAL LEVELS
MAXGEX / MINGEX → static OI walls. Think of them as the monthly structure.

MAXVEX / MINVEX → today's active hedging. More dynamic, follow price action.

MAXCP / MINCP → raw volume imbalance. Where the crowd is positioned right now.
FLOW SIGNALS (ATM)
BAC clusters → sustained call buying. Trend ignition fuel.

BAP clusters → sustained put buying. Hedge accumulation or bear attack.

Mixed BAC + SAP → synthetic long via options. Institutional bullish positioning.

Mixed BAP + SAC → synthetic short. Bearish conviction.
INVENTORY CONTEXT
LONGGAMMA near SPOT → the crowd is long gamma. A big move benefits them — could cascade.

SHORTGAMMA near SPOT → large short-gamma overhang. Breakout = forced covering.

LONGCALLS above SPOT → dealer short delta hedge = selling pressure above.

SHORTPUTS below SPOT → dealer long put = support if tested.
